# LinkedIn Filter System for Automation Opportunities

## Objective
Find companies that already show operational problems on LinkedIn. We are looking for evidence that they have repetitive work, missed leads, slow responses, manual admin, spreadsheet chaos, hiring needs, customer service bottlenecks, scheduling problems, invoicing problems, or CRM problems.

## Ethical use
- Use public LinkedIn posts, jobs, and company pages.
- Do not scrape private profiles or bypass LinkedIn limits.
- Do not spam. Send personalized messages only when there is a clear business reason.
- Save the exact evidence URL and quote so your outreach is specific.

---

# The 5 Main LinkedIn Filters

## Filter 1: Job Post Pain Filter

### What to search
Go to LinkedIn Jobs and search roles that often mean repetitive work exists.

### Job titles to search
- Operations Assistant
- Administrative Assistant
- Data Entry Clerk
- Customer Service Representative
- Appointment Setter
- Receptionist
- Office Manager
- Inventory Coordinator
- Sales Development Representative
- CRM Specialist
- Patient Coordinator
- Client Success Assistant
- Billing Specialist
- Accounts Receivable Clerk
- Dispatcher

### Why this works
If a company is hiring someone to do repetitive admin work, part of that work may be automated.

### What to save in the spreadsheet
- Company name
- Job post URL
- Job title
- Exact responsibilities from the job description
- Problem category
- Automation idea

### Example
Job post says: “Manage incoming emails, update spreadsheets, schedule appointments, follow up with clients.”

Possible automation:
- Form intake
- Email classification
- Auto calendar scheduling
- CRM update
- Follow-up reminders

## Filter 2: Company Post Pain Filter

### What to search
Use LinkedIn search for posts using pain keywords.

### Search examples
- `"manual process" "small business"`
- `"too many emails" company`
- `"missed calls" clinic`
- `"appointment reminders" dental`
- `"spreadsheet" operations`
- `"we are growing" "operations"`
- `"hiring" "admin assistant"`
- `"customer support" "response time"`
- `"inventory" "spreadsheet"`
- `"lead follow up" real estate`

### What to look for
- Founders talking about growth problems
- Companies mentioning operational bottlenecks
- Posts about hiring because the team is overwhelmed
- Comments from employees about manual workflows

- Recruiting agencies

## Filter 4: Growth Signal Filter

Companies that are growing often have messy processes.

### Search terms
- “we are hiring”
- “we are growing”
- “new location”
- “expanding our team”
- “opening soon”
- “now accepting clients”
- “high demand”
- “fully booked”
- “busy season”

### Automation ideas from growth signals
- New lead intake form
- CRM setup
- Automated onboarding
- Appointment reminders
- Internal task dashboard
- Automated quote generator

## Filter 5: Decision Maker Filter

After finding a company, find a person who can say yes.

### Good titles
- Founder
- Owner
- CEO
- COO
- Operations Manager
- Practice Manager
- Office Manager
- Sales Manager
- Head of Customer Success
- General Manager

### Avoid at first
- Huge companies with many departments
- People with no connection to operations or budget
- Very junior employees

# LinkedIn Research Process

## Step 1: Search
Choose one industry and one pain type.

Example:
- Industry: dental clinics
- Pain: missed calls / appointment scheduling

## Step 2: Collect evidence
Save only companies where you can point to specific evidence:
- Job description
- LinkedIn post
- Company announcement
- Founder post
- Hiring trend

## Step 3: Add to spreadsheet
Use `03_Company_Research_Sheet_Template.csv`.

## Step 4: Score
Use the 0-10 scoring system. Contact companies with score 7+.

## Step 5: Create automation angle
Write one specific automation idea, not a generic offer.

Bad:
> I can help with AI automation.

Good:
> I noticed you are hiring an admin assistant to handle scheduling, follow-ups, and spreadsheets. I think a simple intake + scheduling + CRM automation could remove 5-10 hours/week of manual work.

## Step 6: Send outreach
Use a template from `05_Outreach_Message_Templates.md`.

# Best first filters to use

If you want the fastest results, start with:

1. LinkedIn Jobs → `Administrative Assistant` + your city
2. LinkedIn Jobs → `Customer Service Representative` + your city
3. LinkedIn Jobs → `Appointment Setter` + your city
4. LinkedIn Posts → `"hiring" "operations assistant"`
5. LinkedIn Posts → `"spreadsheet" "operations"`

These reveal repetitive work very fast.
