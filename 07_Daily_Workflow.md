# Daily LinkedIn Research Workflow

Use this routine for 60-90 minutes per day.

---

# Daily goal

Find:
- 10 companies
- 5 scored opportunities
- 2 high-priority outreach messages

---

# Step 1: Choose one niche for the day — 5 minutes

Pick one:
- Dental clinics
- Real estate agencies
- Restaurants/catering
- Recruiting agencies
- Construction/home services
- Logistics/delivery
- Gyms/fitness
- Beauty salons/med spas
- E-commerce stores

Do not research every industry at once.

---

# Step 2: Search LinkedIn Jobs — 20 minutes

Search job titles:
- Administrative Assistant
- Operations Assistant
- Customer Service Representative
- Appointment Setter
- Receptionist
- Office Manager
- Inventory Coordinator

Save companies where the job post mentions:
- scheduling
- emails
- spreadsheets
- CRM
- data entry
- follow-ups
- invoices
- customer support

---

# Step 3: Search LinkedIn Posts — 20 minutes

Use pain terms:
- “we are hiring” + industry
- “spreadsheet” + industry
- “missed calls” + industry
- “appointment reminders” + industry
- “lead follow up” + industry
- “operations assistant” + industry

Save public posts with clear evidence.

---

# Step 4: Find decision maker — 10 minutes

Look for:
- Owner
- Founder
- CEO
- COO
- Operations Manager
- Office Manager
- Practice Manager

Save name/title in the spreadsheet.

---

# Step 5: Score each company — 10 minutes

Use `04_Scoring_System.md`.

Only contact:
- 8-10 = contact today
- 6-7 = contact later or if very relevant

---

# Step 6: Write personalized outreach — 15 minutes

Use `05_Outreach_Message_Templates.md`.

Every message must include:
1. Specific LinkedIn signal
2. Specific problem
3. Specific automation idea
4. Offer for free 1-page report

---

# Weekly target

Per week:
- 50 companies researched
- 20 scored opportunities
- 10 outreach messages
- 3-5 replies
- 1-2 discovery calls

---

# First month target

Month 1 goal:
- 200 companies researched
- 40-60 strong opportunities
- 40 personalized outreaches
- 5-10 calls
- 1 first paid pilot project

Start with a small project. Do not try to sell a huge system first.
